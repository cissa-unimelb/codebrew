About

Codebrew welcomes all students regardless of skill level to work through an exhilarating and rewarding 36 hours in a team environment, to experience the joy of coming up with innovative ideas and turning them into reality.

With the goal of our hackathon being to develop the participants’ technical knowledge, we will also be holding workshops on topics related to UI/UX design, web development, version control etc. leading up to and during the event. Experienced mentors from industry leaders such as Google, IMC, Microsoft, etc. will also be present to provide participants with guidance and offer expert opinions on their ideas.

How to Sign Up?

  1. 1. Purchase a ticket from Eventbrite.
  2. 2. Join our Discord server.
  3. 3. If you don't have a team, don't worry! You can form or join teams in the #team-finder channel on Discord.
  4. 4. Once you have a team & individually purchased tickets, fill out the team registration form (one per team).
  5. 5. You're all set for Codebrew 2021!

Eligibility

  • All current university students are welcome to compete!
  • Teams must comprise of 4-5 people.* Teams with less than the required number will be asked to merge. Codebrew organisers reserve the right to relax this restriction at our discretion.
    • * Whilst the event serves as an incentive for cross-university collaboration for students, we strongly encourage all teams to find at least one University of Melbourne student. We would ask you to make use of our Discord to seek out a collaborator. If not, the team, regardless of whether they are from the University of Melbourne, are eligible to receive the prize although you may be asked to show evidence of finding a University of Melbourne member. The prizes include winning the Hackathon (in full) and the respective prize draws.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Codebrew?

Codebrew is an annual hackathon run by students, for students. Hack together a prototype based on a theme over the course of 36 hours.

Who can attend?

Any university student can attend Codebrew!

When is it?

Codebrew will be held from the 16th (Friday) to the 19th (Monday) of April.

What is the theme?

This year's theme will be on preparing for the post-COVID era!

What is the team size?

4-5 people per team.

How much is a ticket?

$5 per head.

Can the Care Pack be delivered?

The Care Pack can only be delivered to participants in Australia but not in Melbourne.

Why should I go?

Meet like-minded students, network with industry leaders, give yourself an edge when applying for internships and grad jobs, win prizes and make a difference!

Who runs Codebrew?

Codebrew is run by CISSA, the premier computing club at the University of Melbourne.
