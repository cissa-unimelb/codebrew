CISSA x MUHI Cobebrew 2020

for the Front-Line and Life-Line

This year we have seen the rapid adoption of medical technologies by the healthcare community as a result of the global impact of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, and we believe that the intersections between these two industries will only become more prevalent in the years to come. It is time for university students to come together in teams to build, innovate and compete to make the best technological solutions to a pressing topic this year. CISSA will be collaborating with MUHI to deliver the Codebrew Health Hackathon this 2020. Show off your skills, solve real problems and compete for glory (and prizes!) at our annual Codebrew hackathon!

  • Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Codebrew?

    Codebrew is an annual hackathon run by students, for students. Form teams of 4 to 6 and hack together a prototype based on a theme over the course of 27 hours.

  • Who can attend?

    Any university student can attend Codebrew!

  • When is it?

    Codebrew will be held from the 25TH (Friday) to the 27th (Sunday) of September. The 'hack' will commence from Friday evening to Sunday morning.

  • Why should I go?

    Meet like-minded students, network with industry leaders, give yourself an edge when applying for internships and grad jobs, win prizes and make a difference!

  • What is the theme?

    The theme this year is related to medical technology. Specific topics will be released at a later date!

  • What is the team size?

    4-6 people per team.

  • Do I need a team to signup?

    Not at all! You can still signup and form/join teams on our slack channel later. Checkout the signup & submission section for more details

  • Who runs Codebrew?

    This year's Codebrew hackathon is run by CISSA and MUHI - with the support of our generous sponsors. CISSA is the Computing and Information Systems Students Association at the University of Melbourne. MUHI is the Melbourne University Health Initiative.
