Codebrew is CISSA’s annual flagship hackathon for students. The hackathon will take place over the weekend, where students will form teams of 4-5, to create a software project under a given theme. This involves coming up with an idea, writing the software, and pitching their product to a panel of judges (As Codebrew this year will be virtual, we will be doing video submissions).

With a theme of “preparing for the post COVID era”, participants are asked to brainstorm ideas under 3 different tracks : Remote work and education, Social good, and Mental Health. The goal of the hackathon is to develop the participants’ technical knowledge, and also try to come up with technological solutions to contemporary issues in this ever changing world.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Codebrew?

Codebrew is an annual hackathon run by students, for students. Form teams of 4 to 6 and hack together a prototype based on a theme over the course of 27 hours.

Who can attend?

Any university student can attend Codebrew!

When is it?

Codebrew will be held from the 16th (Friday) to the 18th (Sunday) of April.

What is the theme?

Relevant to the recent COVID-19 pandemic, this year's theme will be on remote work & education, social good, and mental health!

What is the team size?

4-6 people per team.

How much is a ticket?

$15 per head.

Why should I go?

Meet like-minded students, network with industry leaders, give yourself an edge when applying for internships and grad jobs, win prizes and make a difference!

Who runs Codebrew?

Codebrew is run by CISSA, the premier computing club at the University of Melbourne.
