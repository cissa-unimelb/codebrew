  • Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Codebrew?

    Codebrew is an annual hackathon run by students, for students. Form teams of 4 to 6 and hack together a prototype based on a theme over the course of 27 hours.

  • Who can attend?

    Any university student can attend Codebrew! While the event will be held within the University of Melbourne campus, we invite students from other universities to participate.

  • When is it?

    Codebrew will be held from the 12th (Friday) to the 14th (Sunday) of April. The 'hack' will commence from Saturday morning to Sunday afternoon.

  • Where will it be held at?

    While we will confirm the exact location very soon (check this space!), Codebrew will be held somewhere at the University of Melbourne campus in Parkville, VIC.

  • Why should I go?

    Meet like-minded students, network with industry leaders, give yourself an edge when applying for internships and grad jobs, get free swag, win prizes and make a difference!

  • Do I need a team?

    Not at all! While you can sign up with teammates from the beginning, you can also sign up on your own and form/join teams on Friday night.

  • Do we stay overnight?

    You're welcome to stay overnight or go home and come back in the morning. We encourage you to decide this as a team before the 'hack' starts so that you can be prepared.

  • Who runs Codebrew?

    Codebrew is run by CISSA - the Computing and Information Systems Students Association at the University of Melbourne - with the support of our generous sponsors.

    We run exciting events and promote opportunities just like this! join us as a member and follow us on social media (top right of this page) so you won't miss out!

  • Why isn't this event free?

    CISSA is a non-for-profit association. To run large events like Codebrew we need funding to cover expenses of the hackathon.

    The cost of your ticket covers everything from venue, food, prizes, to t-shirts.
